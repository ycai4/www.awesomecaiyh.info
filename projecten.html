PROJECTS

Project | 01

2015.1 - present

Modern Technoligies make it easy for everybody to manipulate digital da-
ta on the Internet, especially the digital images. And there comes a rising 
doubt on the authenticity of the digital sources. Our team aim to set up a 
smart mechanism to detect manipulations made to digital images. In this 
project, I focused on the Rotation Invariant Localization Algorithm, 
which stems from the Zernike Moment mathimatical attribute. This novel 
method can deal with a huge bunch of digital images quickly successively, 
especially effective to detect the Self Copy(-Rotate)-Move Manipulations 
in digital images. With some advanced image compression techniques, like 
JPEG compression algorithm,  I code with Python language and Matlab to 
realize this magic mechanism.  This is an ongoing project, and we are still 
making progress. If you are interested in the uncomplished project, please 
take a look.

Project | 02

2014.9-2014.12

GPS technology has be widely involved in personal mobile devices. Howe-
ver, the navigation algorithms used in mobile devices are kind of inefficient. 
In order to explore new method, I grouped up with two other fellow students 
in SUNY to aplly the Moore's and Dijkstra's methods learned in math class to 
comparion, hoping to improve the current navigation algorithms. In this proj-
ect, we used the MATLAB to construct a nxn simulation map model, and set 
random start and finish points with some obstacles in the map, to find out the 
shortest path between two points. Although the outcome turned to be of a little 
bit failure, if you are interested in it, you can take a look at our report.
